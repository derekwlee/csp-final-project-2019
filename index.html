What Are Seasonal Allergies?

As human beings, our immune systems have evolved to be able to detect harmful substances in our bodies. However, there are times when our immune systems turn the tables against us and reacts towards harmless substances, such as pollen, dust mites, mold, bee venom, pet dander, latex, and foods. Seasonal allergies, also known as hay fever, specifically occur when a person's immune system detects the increased pollen levels in the air. This is the reason why symptoms of allergies resemble those of a common cold.

The pollen associated with seasonal allergies originates from different trees, grasses, and weeds, and the severity can depend on the season. The severity and type of pollen in the air mainly depends on the season. Over 67 million Americans of all ages suffer from seasonal allergies, so you're not alone! :)

How Do I Know If I Have Seasonal Allergies?

Symptoms of having seasonal allergies include:

  • Sneezing
  • Runny/Stuffy Nose
  • Red/itchy/puffy/watery eyes
  • Mouth/throat itchiness
  • Wheezing
  • Coughing
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Chest tightness
  • Fatigue
  • Headaches
  • General Suffering

Having seasonal allergies can also consequently result in food allergies to foods and vegetables. Sometimes, the body's immune system can face trouble differing between proteins in fruits/vegetables and pollen. This causes certain fruits and vegetables to cross-react with certain pollen that a person is allergic to. This phenomenon is called Oral Allergy Syndrome (OAS).

How Can I Treat My Allergies?

There are various ways to treat seasonal allergies. For one, reducing exposure to the allergen is the biggest step. By closing the windows, using air conditioning, staying indoors during high pollen count, and regularly washing clothing/hands, there's a much lesser risk for a person's immune system to encounter pollen.

However, there are also times where it's near impossible to avoid pollen. Medicines such as antihistamines, decongestants, and nasal sprays can help in this regard. However, if a person is facing severe debilitating allergies, it's highly recommended that they contact a medical professional. Most likely, the professional will recommend allergy shots to help desensitize the person from their allergen.
